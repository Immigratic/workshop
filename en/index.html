Arrival in Toronto

Your business trip will commence upon your arrival at Toronto Airport on June 27. A representative from Immigratic will greet you at the airport and accompany you to your hotel located in Niagara Falls, offering a stunning view of the waterfall. A specialized SIM card and travel brochure will be provided to you.

Stay in Niagara

The following day (June 28), after enjoying a breakfast with a view of one of the world’s most beautiful waterfalls and taking a stroll in the beautiful area surrounding Niagara Falls, we proceed on a boat tour of Niagara Falls. The group’s program for the second day concludes with lunch at a beautiful, rotating restaurant in Niagara.

Stay in Toronto

On June 29, after breakfast and hotel check-out, we move to the metropolis of Toronto. You will be hosted in one of the city's finest hotels. The same day, after settling into your Toronto hotel rooms, we embark on a half-day city tour of downtown Toronto, including a visit to the iconic CN Tower.

Departure to Ottawa

On June 30, after having breakfast and checking out of the hotel in Toronto, we will head to the city's shopping centers for some retail therapy. We'll then proceed to Toronto's Iranian Plaza for lunch at one of the Iranian restaurants. After lunch, we will leave Toronto and head to Ottawa, where you will stay in a luxurious hotel in Kanata, home to Canada’s largest technology park.

Stay in Ottawa

Canada Day July 1st, which coincides with Canada Day, will see us heading to downtown Ottawa. We will be there till late in the evening to enjoy the fireworks. Lunch will be at another Iranian restaurant in the city.

First Day of the Workshop

The workshop on Canadian Entrepreneurial Immigration starts at 9 AM on July 2nd, providing an overview of Canada's entrepreneurship programs. Representatives from accelerator companies and venture capitalists will introduce their services. A brief interlude at 10:30 AM will allow for individual consultations on immigration, housing, investment, and education. The first day concludes with a summary of Canada's federal entrepreneurship startup program. From noon to 3 PM, special advisory panels will be held in various fields. From 3 to 5 PM, those interested can partake in a bank account opening session facilitated by one of the local banks.

Second Day of the Workshop in Ottawa

On July 3rd, the workshop continues from 9 AM to 12 PM, focusing on establishing your business branches in Canada. Consultations with loan experts for residential and commercial loans, as well as a review of government grants for businesses, will be discussed. We'll then proceed to downtown Ottawa for lunch and continue the process of bank account openings.

Last Day of the Workshop

July 4th, the final day of the workshop, runs from 9 AM to 12 PM and focuses on Ontario's provincial entrepreneurship programs. The last session deals with the conditions for family members regarding education and work in Canada during the immigration process. Special panels continue from 12 to 2 PM. Afterward, we will visit some ready-to-sell businesses in Ottawa.

Review of Ready-to-Sell Businesses

On July 5th, those interested in purchasing a ready-to-sell business can continue their tours in Ottawa. Others can enjoy a free day exploring the city and its shopping centers.

Departure to Montreal

On July 6th, we will head to the tourist city of Montreal after breakfast and hotel checkout. You'll stay at a central hotel in Montreal. Upon arrival, you can enjoy a romantic evening at the cafes and restaurants of Old Montreal.

Return to Iran

On July 7th, we'll check out of the hotel and make our way to the Montreal airport for our flight back to Tehran, concluding our journey.

General Requirements for Participants

Since the content of this event focuses on entrepreneurial and business immigration programs, to elevate the quality level of the event and facilitate the visa process, as a general rule, eligible applicants must have:
  • A minimum of three years of business ownership or senior management experience.
  • A minimum net asset value of CAD 800,000.
  • At least CAD 100,000 of liquid capital readily available.
  • A minimum willingness to invest CAD 200,000 over the next 2 years.
  • Directly or indirectly supervise at least 2 employees or personnel.
  • Have some level of proficiency or understanding of the English language.
  • Higher or relevant education, residential and commercial properties, travel history to the Schengen area, the U.S., the U.K., and Canada will be considered as a positive attribute
